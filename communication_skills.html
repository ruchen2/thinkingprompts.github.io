Develop Effective Communication Skills

  • Self-Disclosure

    • Acknowledge ownership of your statements (usually begin with I think, I feel, or I believe)
    • Make sense statements (seeing, hearing, smelling, tasting,and touching)
    • Use interpretive statements (supply information you took into account before making the statement)
    • Make feeling statements (reveal information about your comfort level and the reasons behind your reactions)
      • Tune in to your feelings
      • Accurately convey your feelings (I feel (happy, angry, sad, fearful, disgusted, surprised ...)
      • Be sensitive to the respondent (choose appropriate time, place, and what feelings to disclose)
    • Use intention statements
      • Reveal your desires (generally begin with I want, I would like, or I wish)
      • Be judicious on the desires that you reveal. Generally, you don't want to reveal your hidden agenda
      • Be sensitive to the other person's intentions
    • Make action statements
      • Inform a person why you did, or will do, or are doing something
      • Chronicle that you did or are doing it
      • Be aware of the possible impact your action might have

  • Assertiveness

    • Use words to promot yur assertiveness
      • Document your position by recalling relevant facts
      • Acknowledge that you understand the other person's position
      • Use repetition
      • Use feeling statements
      • Document the reasons for your position
      • Strive for a compromise
    • Use body language to reinforce your assertiveness
      • Use appropriate positioning (stay in close proximity, stand/sit straight, lean forward, maintain direct eye contact)
      • Use appropriate facial expression
      • Keep control of you voice infection
    • Tune in to aggresssive behavior and passive behavior

  • Dynamic Listening

    • Become aware of your personal filters
      • Predilection filter: Hear only what we want to hear
      • The who filter: place too much importance on who says it
      • The facts filter: all you hear is the facts; oblivious to any emotive messages
      • Distracting thoughts filter: your mind wanders off
    • Become a dynamic listener
      • Summarize the speaker's statements
      • Use acknowledgment phrases (I see, Oh really, I'd like to hear more about it)
      • Acknowlege feeling statements with I-hear statements
      • Use appropriate nonverbal cues (establish direct eye contact, lean forward, nod your head)
    • Tune in to the emotional subcontext

  • Criticism

    • Give criticism productively
      • Recognize that giving criticism can help people
      • Make sure that time and place are appropriate
      • Protect the other person's self-esteem
      • Emphasize improvement
      • Show concern
      • Manage your own emotion
    • Give criticism effectively
      • Identify the particular behavior you are criticizing
      • Identify why it is a problem
      • Assess how best to present the criticism
      • Make a lis of possible changes
      • Point out that you're relating your perception
      • Give specific examples that illustrate the problem
      • Be sure to give positive input
      • Acknowledge the difficulty of the situation
      • Look for clues as to how the other person is responding
      • Discuss possible solutions
      • Reiterate the benefits to be gained
      • End on a positive note
      • Follow up
      • Assess how effective the changes are
    • Receive criticism productively
      • Recognize that you learn something from the criticism
      • Listen carefully before saying anything
      • Tune in to your emotions
      • Take responsibility for your actions
      • Don't become defensive
      • Turn off any listening filters
      • Use self-disclosure statements
      • Use assertiveness (you need to stand up to your ideas, beliefs, and desires when criticism is not quite justified)
      • Be sensitive to what the criticism-giver is feeling
      • Summarize the other person's statements
      • Show willingness to change

  • Team Communication

    • Encourage productive team communication
      • Use and encourage self-disclosure
      • Pratice and encourage dynamic listening
      • Engage in problem solving
      • Use assertiveness and criticism when necessary
    • Facilitate effective team communication
      • Be inclusive
      • Discourage dominance
      • Be supportive. Give positive recognition
      • Keep the emotional tenor at a manageable level
      • Invite disagreement
      • Be aware of how each member participates and responds
