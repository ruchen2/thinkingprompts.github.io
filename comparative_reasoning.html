Recognize Comparative Reasoning

  • "This Is Like That" Thinking
  • Comparative reasoning is the process of using what is more familiar to make interpretation, explanation, or inferences about what is less familiar

Evaluate comparative Reasoning using five criteria

  • Familarity
  • Simplicity
  • Comprehensiveness
  • Productivity
  • Testability

Use comparative Reasoning

  • To make tentative projections
  • To propose hypotheses
  • To offer imprecise probabilistic explanations

Be Aware of Risks of Comparative Inferences

  • They are notoriously weak and unreliable, from a logic point of view
  • They are not acceptable proofs of the truth of their conclusions
