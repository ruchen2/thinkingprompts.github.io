Employ Six Cognitive Skills

  • Interpretation
    • subskills: clarify meaning, categorize, decode significance
    • Questions
    • What does this mean?
    • What's happening?
    • How should we understand that(e.g. what he or she just said)?
    • What is the best way to characterize/categorize/classify this?
    • In this context, what was intended by saying/doing that?
    • How can we make sense out of this (experience, feeling, statement)?
  • Analysis
    • subskills: examine ideas, identify arguments, identify reasons and claims
    • Questions
    • Could you please tell us again your reasons for making that claim?
    • What is your conclusion/WHat is it that you are claiming?
    • Why do you think that?
    • What are the arguments pro and con?
    • What assumptions must have make to accept the conclusion?
    • What is your basis for saying that?
  • Inference
    • subskills: query evidence, conjecture alternatives, draw conclusions using inductive or deductive reasoning
    • Questions
    • Given what we know so far, what conclusions can we draw?
    • Given what we know so far, what can we rule out?
    • What does this evidence imply?
    • If we abandoned/accepted that assumption,how would things change?
    • What additional information do we need to resolve this question?
    • If we believe these things, what would they imply for us going forward?
    • What are the consequence of doing things that way?
    • What are some alternatives we haven't explored?
    • Let's consider each option and see where it takes us.
    • Are there any undesirable consequences that we can and should foresee?
  • Evaluation
    • subskills: assess credibility of claims, assess quality of arguments that were made using inductive or deductive reasoning
    • Questions
    • How credible is that claim?
    • Why do we think we can trust what this person claims?
    • How strong are those arguments?
    • Do we have our facts right?
    • How confident can we be in our conclusion, given what we now know?
  • Explanation
    • subskills: state results, justify procedures, present arguments
    • Questions
    • What were the specific findings/results of the investigation?
    • Could you please tell us how you conducted that analysis?
    • How did you come to that interpretation?
    • Could you please take us through your reasoning one more time?
    • Why do you think that (was the right answer/was the soltuion)?
    • How would you explain why this particular decision was made?
  • Self-Regulatioin
    • subskills: self-monitor, self-correct
    • Questions
    • Our position on this issue is still too vague; can we be more precise?
    • How good was our methodology, and how well did we follow it?
    • Is there a way we reconcile these two apparently conflicting conclusions?
    • How good is our evidence?
    • OK, before we commit, what are we missing?
    • I'm finding some our definitions a little confusing; can we revisit what we mean by certain things before making any final decisions?
