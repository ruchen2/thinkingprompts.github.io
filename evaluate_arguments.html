Evalute Arguments

  • Test an argument's worthiness
    1. Truthfulness of the premises: the reason is true in each of its premises, explicit and implicit
    2. Logical strength: If the reason were true, then the claim/conclusion would be true or very probably true
    3. Relevance: The truth of the claim/conclusion depends on the truth of the reason
    4. Non-circularity: The truth of the reason does not depend on the truth of the claim
  • Be aware of fallacies
  • Recognize common informal fallacies
    • Appeal to ignorance
    • Appeal to the mob (aka. bandwagon fallacy)
    • Appeal to emotion
    • Argument from repetition
    • Argument from silence
    • Ad Hominum attack
    • Straw man fallacy
    • Playing with words
    • Misuse of authority
    • False dilemma
