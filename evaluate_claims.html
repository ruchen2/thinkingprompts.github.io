Evaluate Claims

  • Assess the source - whom should I trust?

    • Twelve characteristics of a trustworthy source

  • Learned in topic X
  • Experienced in topic X
  • Speaking about X
  • Up-to-date about X
  • Capable of explaining the basis for their claim or their advice about X
  • Unbiased
  • Truthful
  • Free of conflicts of interest
  • Acting in accord with our interests
  • Unconstrained
  • Informed aobut the specifics of the case at hand
  • Mentally stable
    •
  • Assess the substance - what should I believe?
    • Detect false claims: self-contradictions and tautologies
    • Be on the alert for unsubstantiated claims: marketing, spin, disinformation, and propaganda
    • Be aware of slanted language, half-truths, exaggerations, and loaded expressions
  • Verify the claim independently
    • Can the claim be confirmed?
    • Can the claim be disconfirmed?
    • Suspend judgment
