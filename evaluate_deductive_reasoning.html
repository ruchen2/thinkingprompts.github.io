Evalute Deductive Reasoning

  • Reason deductively about declarative statements
    • Denying the consequence: (1)If A, then B. (2)Not B. (3)Therefore, not A
    • Affirming the antecedent: (1)If A, then B. (2)A. (3)Therefore, B
    • Disjunctive syllogism: (1)Either A or B. (2)Not A. (3)Therefore, B
  • Reason deductively about classes of objects
    • Applying a generalization: (1)Every member of F is a member of G. (2)Individual object X is a member of F. (3)So, X is a member of G
    • Applying an exception: (1)Every F is a G. (2)The object X is not a G. (3)So, X is not an F
  • Reason deductively about relationships
    • Transitivity: If X has a transitive relationship to Y, and Y has the same transitive reationship to Z, then X has the same transitive relationship to Z
    • Reflectivity: If X has a reflective relationship to Y, then Y has the same reflective relationship to X
    • Identity: If X is Y, then Y is X
  • Fallacies disguising as valid deductive arguments
    • Fallacy of affirming the consequence: (1)If A, then B. (2)B. (3)Therefore, A
    • Fallacy of denying the antecedent: (1)If A, then B. (2)Not A. (3)Therefore, not B
    • Fallacy of false classification: One group is classified as part of the other group because both have the same attribute
    • Fallacy of composition: A group has exactly the same attribute that each of its members have
    • Fallacy of division: Each individual member of a group has the same attribute that the group as a whole has
    • Fallacy of false reference: Two descriptions refer the same thing
