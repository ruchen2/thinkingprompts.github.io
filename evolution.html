Evolution of Technique

    Both the quantity and quality of human needs, as well as requirements for humans, increase with time

    Any technique's evolution brings the increase of its ideality

  • Multiplication: transition from mono-system to bi-system to poly-system
  • Trimming: decrease in number of subsystems for neutral and auxiliary functions
  • Poly-functionality: increased quantity of useful functions by adding new subsystems
  • Aggregation: achieve many functions within or by one subsystem
  • Dehumanization: exclude people from performing a noncreative work through mechanization, automation, and computerization
  • Resource: achieve low cost by use of various resources
  • Minimization: achieve the smallest dimensions, smallest weight, and smallest energy consumption of any subsystem
  • New materials: substitute current materials with new ones with advantageous properties, some of which can provide useful functions
  • Encapsulation: put replaceable subsystems into a more easily handled cassette or cartridge
  • Modular construction: connect the construction elements into suitable groups
  • Standardization: unify the dimensions, forms, and other properties and values of subsystems
  • Typification: establish the optimum number of variants and sizes of selected characteristic properties of a particular technique
  • Reuse: use subsystems that have already been designed and tested for a previous application as some of the subsystems of the new system
  • Recycling: use elements of dead technique as newe materials in new technique

  • Each technique and each subsystem has its own representative time of evolution

    Any technique with nonsingle subsystems has a few time scale during technique development

  • Uniformity: The uneven development of subsystems leads to existence of contradictions in a technique
  • Completeness: a tehnical system will not work if one important subsystem is missing or does not perform well
  • Transparency: a technique will not work if one of the energy/sustance/information links is missing or does not perform well

Paths of Evolution

  • Physical states: solid - liquid - gas - plasma - field - vacuum
  • Interaction: continuous - vibration - resonance
  • Dimensionality: zero D - 1D - 2D - 3D
  • Adaptability: rigid - dynamic - multi-hinges - elastic - soft - field flexibility
  • Voidness: monolith - inhomogenous solid - solid with voids - solid with capillaries - porous solid - solid with dispersed micro-voids
  • Human involvement: mechnization - automation - computerization
