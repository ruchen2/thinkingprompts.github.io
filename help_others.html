Help Others Help Themselves

  • Keep your emotional perspective
    • Emotions can be contagious
    • Here's what you can do to prevent another person's negative emotions from infecting you
      • Anticipate the other person's emotional state
      • Tune in to the other person's behavior
      • Use instructive statements
      • Use relaxation techniques
  • Calm an out-of-control person
    • Suggest to take a seat
    • Offer something to drink
    • Ask to speak more slowly
    • Give a creative time-out
  • Be a supportive listener
    • Acknowledge your acceptance of what the speaker is saying
    • Help the speaker to clarify thoughts, ideas, and feelings
      • Repeat what the person says
      • Paraphrase what you think the person really means
      • Share your perceptions of the person's feeling
        • Avoid implying that you are making a judgment
        • Avoid suggesting that your are making a negative evaluation
        • Avoid challenging statements
        • Repeatedly verify the accuracy of your perceptions
      • Ask purposeful questions (three levels: open-ended, direct, and specific)
  • Help others plan their goals and reach them
    • Help the person draw up a contract
    • Use modeling and role playing
    • Reinforce positive steps
      • Be consistent
      • Reward the behavior as soon as possible after it it manifested
      • Define the cause-and-effect relationship
      • Be sure the reinforcement is meaningful
