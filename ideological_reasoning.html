Recognize ideological reasoning

  • "Top Down" Thinking
  • Ideological reasoning is the process of thinking that begins with abstractions or generalization that express one's core beliefs, concepts, values, or principles and proceed to reason top-down to specific applications
  • Ideological reasoning is deductivee in character
  • Ideological premises are axiomatic
  • The argument maker takes the ideological absolutes on faith

Evaluate ideological reasoning

  • Are all the premises true?
  • Is the argument logically strong?
  • Are the reasons relavent to the claim being made?
  • Is the argument non-circular?

Benefits of ideological reasoning

  • Ideologies define and shape communities and give individuals a strong sense of identity
  • Ideological convictions enable people to escape the suffocating malaise of relativism
  • Ideological reasoning offers an efficient way of addressing novel questions

Risks of ideological reasoning

  • Ideological belief structures are normative - those who diagree are seen as outsiders, ignorant, mistaken, or dangerous
  • Ideological convictions generally operate unconscieously, and subtly influence people's interpretations of events and experiences
  • Ideological convictions often seem to be immunized against scientific confirmation or discomformation, it can be difficult for people to see them as mistaken
