Develop Interpersonal Expertise

  • What makes a relationship
    • Meeting each other's needs
    • Relating to each other over time
    • Exchanging information about one's thoughts, ideas, and feelings
  • How to analyze a relationship
    • Know the relationship boundaries
    • Consider relationship expectations
    • Examine your perceptions of the other person
    • Ascertain the other person's perceptions of you
    • Examine specific encounters
    • Determine desired relationship outcomes
  • How to communicate at appropriate levels
    • Niceties
    • Factual information
    • Thoughts and ideas
    • Feelings
  • How to use the different levels effectively
    • Identify the level you are each at
    • Match your level to the other person's
    • Know when and how to move to a different level
      • Use self-disclosure statements
      • Ask strategic questions
      • Solicit thoughts and feelings
      • Solicit thoughts and feelings about expressing thoughts and feelings
    • Know when and when not to shift levels
      • Shift to a factual information level if someone is extremely angry
      • Shift to a feeling level when someone communicates despondency nonverbally
      • Don't shift out of the factual or the thoughts and ideas level when you're doing problem solving
      • Don't shift out of the niceties level if you're not prepared to have a more meaningful exchange
      • Generally don't shift to a level different from the other person's if he/she is in a position of power or control
