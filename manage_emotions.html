Manage Your Emotions

  • Take charge of your thoughts
    • Tune into your automatic thoughts
      • Don't overgeneralize
      • Stay away from destructive labeling
      • Avoid mind reading
      • Don't have rules about how others should act
      • Don't inflate the significance of an event
    • Develop constructive inner dialogues
      • Imagine a situation you are likely to encounter
      • Write down instructive statement you can use to help you effectively manage your emotion in the situation
  • Manage your physiological changes (arousal)
    • Acknowledge physiological changes (heart rate, blood pressure, respiration rate, and perspiration rate)
    • Use relaxation to decrease your arousal (conditioned relaxation response, meditation, yoga, etc.)
  • Take control of your behaviour patterns
    • Recognize your behaviors (associated with emotions: anger, joy, anxiety, contentment, depression, enthusiasm, fear, confidence, sadness, frustration)
    • Derail counterproductive behaviors (sarcasm, avoidance, boastfulness, etc.)
      • Take deep breaths
      • Engage in a constructive inner dialogue
  • Become a good problem solver
    • Identify and define the problem situation
    • Change you perspection of the situation
    • Generate alternative solutions
    • Explore different options
    • Define the best strategies
    • Evaluate the results
  • Additional techniques for managing your emotions
    • Use humor (funny passages/quotations, silly photoes, videos, shows, movies, jokes, experience)
    • Redirect your emotion energy (take up some simple tasks and busywork: ordering supplied, dusting, cleaning desk, etc.)
    • Take time out
    • Manage anger
      • Communicate your anger
      • State your wish to remove yourself temporarily from the situation
      • Remove yourself immediately
      • Use the time-out productiviely
