Motivate Yourself

  • Motivated by yourself (your thoughts, arousal, and behaviors)
    • Become a positive thinker
      • Use motivational self-statement (fortify your confidence, optimism, tenacity, enthusiasm, and resilience)
      • Play mental games (Day One, Best Workday, etc.)
      • Focus your thoughts with time lock and focal lock (overcome procrastination, avoidance, and inertia)
        • time lock is a period of time that you block off for doing intense work
        • focal lock is a list of tasks to be done during time lock
      • Use mental imagery (imagine yourself struggling with the task, regaining your composure, succeeding, and feeling good)
      • Use productive self-criticism (acknowlege progress/accomplishment and identify rooms for improvement)
      • Give yourself meaningful goals
    • Use arousal to generate energy
      • Move around
      • Relax (take a bath, read a book, listen to music, meditate, take a catnap, etc.)
    • Utilize productive actions and behaviors
      • Break work down into small tasks
      • Mointor your behaviors (examine how they affect your motivation and decide which ones to continue doing and which ones to discard)
  • Motivated by supporting family, friends, and colleagues
    • Develop mutually motivational relationships
    • What to look for: trust, suitability, availability
    • What to ask for: sense of perspective, solutions to problem
    • How to reciprocate: draw upon one another for motivational support, express appreciation, maintain connection
  • Motivated by an emotional mentor (a real or fictious person)
    • Choose as your mentor an individual who is extraordinarily motivating for you
    • Choose a motivational moment in that person's life, and relive it often
    • Call upon you mentor often
  • Motivated by your environment
    • Make it as healthy and helpful as possible
      • Breath clean air
      • Tune in helpful sounds; tune out hindering sounds
      • Use light advantageously
    • Surround yourself with motivationally supportive objects (photos, magazine clippings, inspirational sayings or phrases, visual cues, and reminder notes)
    • Organize it so it works for you rather than against you
  • Deal with setbacks and create comebacks (7 stages)
    1. Disbelief: use self-awareness skills
    2. Anger: use emotion management skills
    3. Yearning to turn back time
    4. Depression: use self-motivation skills
    5. Acceptance: use self-awareness skills
    6. Hope
    7. Positive Activity: use self-motivation skills
